In New Zealand, "escrow" is not governed by a single, dedicated "Escrow Act." Instead, it is a contractual arrangement regulated by a patchwork of laws depending on the nature of the assets and the identity of the escrow agent.The following overview outlines the legal codes, parameters, and requirements for parties involved in an escrow arrangement in Aotearoa New Zealand.1. The Legal Framework and CodesBecause escrow is essentially a specialized trust or bailment, it is governed by:Contract and Commercial Law Act 2017: Governs the underlying sale and purchase agreements and the contractual terms of the escrow.Trusts Act 2019: Applies if the escrow agent is deemed a trustee. It sets out "mandatory duties" (e.g., duty to act honestly and in good faith) that cannot be contracted out of.Lawyers and Conveyancers Act 2006: If a lawyer acts as the escrow agent, they must follow strict Trust Account Rules. Section 110 mandates that all client money must be held in a trust account and accounted for strictly.  Financial Markets Conduct Act 2013 (FMC Act): Relevant if the escrow involves financial products (shares, derivatives) or occurs within a regulated investment scheme. Escrow agents in these contexts may need to be licensed "custodians."2. Roles and Duties of Each PartyAn escrow typically involves three parties: the Depositor (Buyer), the Beneficiary (Seller), and the Escrow Agent.A. The Depositor (Buyer)Duty: Must deposit the funds or assets into the designated escrow account by the agreed date.Requirement: Must provide proof of funds and comply with Anti-Money Laundering (AML) verification.Risk: If they fail to deposit, they are usually in breach of the main commercial contract.B. The Beneficiary (Seller)Duty: Must fulfill the "trigger conditions" (e.g., delivering a product, transferring a domain, or completing a service).Requirement: Must provide evidence of fulfillment to the Escrow Agent as specified in the agreement.C. The Escrow Agent (The Neutral Third Party)Role: An independent stakeholder who holds the asset.  Duties:Impartiality: Must not favor either party.  Strict Adherence: Can only release funds if the precise, pre-defined conditions are met.Verification: Responsible for confirming that conditions (like a courier delivery or code handover) have actually occurred.3. Parameters and Mandatory ClausesA valid New Zealand escrow agreement should cover these parameters:Clause CategoryRequirement / ParameterTrigger EventsMust be objective. For example: "Release funds 24 hours after a New Zealand Post tracking number shows 'Delivered'."Time LimitsA "Longstop Date" after which funds are returned to the buyer if conditions aren't met.Dispute ResolutionDefines what happens if the buyer says "it's broken" and the seller says "it's fine." Usually involves holding funds until a court order or independent mediation.Fees & InterestWho pays the agent’s fee (often 50/50) and who keeps the interest earned while money sits in the account.AML/CFTCompliance with the Anti-Money Laundering and Countering Financing of Terrorism Act 2009. The agent must verify identities before accepting money.4. Specific Rules for the Escrow AgentThe person or entity "doing the escrowing" has the highest legal burden:Segregation of Funds: Escrowed money must never be mixed with the agent's personal or business operating cash.Record Keeping: Under the Trusts Act 2019, agents should keep records for the duration of the trust and at least a year after it ends.Limited Liability: Most agents include "Exculpatory Clauses" stating they are not liable for anything except their own "gross negligence" or "willful default."Interpleader Actions: If parties argue, the agent has the right to "interplead"—meaning they hand the money over to a New Zealand court and let a judge decide, removing themselves from the fight.Important Note for Peer-to-Peer/Courier Escrow  If you are setting this up for a delivery where parties cannot meet:Written Consent: Both parties must sign a document (digital is fine) agreeing to the specific "Release Trigger" (e.g., the courier's signature).Disclosure: The agent must tell the buyer exactly how long the "hold" is valid and tell the seller exactly what proof is needed to get paid.
